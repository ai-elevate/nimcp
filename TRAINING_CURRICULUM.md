# NIMCP Training Curriculum
## Human-Inspired Developmental Learning Schedule

**Philosophy**: Train like a human infant → adult (simple → complex)
**Duration**: 5 phases over ~100-200 hours of training
**Total Examples**: ~5-10 million examples
**Cognitive Systems**: All active (curiosity, skepticism, attention, memory consolidation)
**Domains**: 28 total (Sociology, Anthropology, History, Art, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Conflict, Religion, Mythology, Linguistics, Politics, Oceanography, Philosophy, Latin, Greek, Rhetoric, Law, Meta-law, **Mathematics**, **Software Engineering**, **Electrical Engineering**, **Psychiatry**, **Medicine**, **Finance**, **Data Science**, **Current Affairs**, **Cooking**)

**IMPORTANT NOTE ON "READING"**: The brain doesn't need to learn character recognition or basic literacy. Feature extraction handles that automatically (character frequencies, n-grams, embeddings). The brain learns **concepts, patterns, and knowledge**, not pixel-to-text decoding. Think of it as: the brain already knows how to "read" - now it's learning what the words *mean*.

---

## 🧠 LEARNING STAGES (Biological Inspiration)

NIMCP's curiosity engine defines these developmental stages:
- **INFANT** (0-2 years): Sensory learning, basic patterns
- **TODDLER** (2-4 years): Language acquisition, simple concepts
- **CHILD** (4-12 years): Structured learning, reading, writing
- **ADOLESCENT** (12-18 years): Abstract reasoning, complex domains
- **ADULT** (18+ years): Specialized knowledge, expertise
- **EXPERT** (Professional): Deep expertise, cross-domain synthesis

## 📅 PHASE 1: INFANT STAGE (Foundation)
**Duration**: 20-30 hours | **Examples**: 1-2 million
**Curiosity Level**: 0.95 (extremely high - everything is novel)
**Learning Rate**: 0.02 (high plasticity)
**Consolidation**: Every 500 examples (frequent sleep cycles)

### Cognitive Focus:
- **Primary**: Sensory-motor patterns, basic associations
- **Attention**: Short-term (immediate stimuli)
- **Working Memory**: Low capacity (1-2 items)
- **Skepticism**: Low (accept most information - building priors)

### Domains Introduced (SIMPLE FIRST - Like Teaching a Baby):

**Start with concrete, observable, physical concepts:**

1. **Biology/Medicine - Observable Life & Health** (22% of phase)
   - **Simple**: Animals you can see (dog, cat, bird)
   - **Simple**: Body parts (hand, eye, mouth, heart beats)
   - **Simple**: Basic needs (hungry, thirsty, tired, sleep)
   - **Simple**: Healthy vs sick (feels good vs feels bad)
   - Datasets: iNaturalist (images), children's biology books, basic health

2. **Art - Visual Patterns** (20% of phase)
   - **Simple**: Colors (red, blue, green)
   - **Simple**: Shapes (circle, square, triangle)
   - **Simple**: Patterns (stripes, dots)
   - Datasets: WikiArt (simple images), children's art

3. **Mathematics - Counting & Shapes** (20% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: Numbers 1-10 (counting objects)
   - **Simple**: More/less, big/small
   - **Simple**: Geometric shapes
   - **Simple**: Simple addition (1+1=2)
   - Datasets: Basic arithmetic, shape recognition

4. **Physics - Physical World** (15% of phase)
   - **Simple**: Objects fall down (gravity)
   - **Simple**: Push/pull (force)
   - **Simple**: Hot/cold (temperature)
   - Datasets: PhET simulations (simple), physics toys

5. **Linguistics - First Words** (15% of phase)
   - **Simple**: Common nouns (mom, dad, dog, ball)
   - **Simple**: Action words (eat, sleep, run)
   - **Simple**: Single words → two-word phrases
   - Datasets: Common Voice (simple), baby talk corpus

6. **Anthropology/Sociology - Family** (4% of phase)
   - **Simple**: Family members (mother, father, baby)
   - Datasets: Family structure descriptions

6b. **Psychiatry - Emotions & Feelings** (3% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: Basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, scared)
   - **Simple**: How feelings work (when happy, smile; when sad, cry)
   - **Simple**: Comfort and care (hug makes feel better)
   - Datasets: Emotion recognition datasets, children's emotional development

7. **Software Engineering - Computing Basics** (3% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: What is a computer?
   - **Simple**: Input/output (keyboard, screen, mouse)
   - **Simple**: On/off, yes/no (binary basics)
   - Datasets: Basic computing tutorials, CS Unplugged

8. **Electrical Engineering - Power & Light** (2% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: Electricity makes things work
   - **Simple**: Battery, wire, light bulb
   - **Simple**: On/off switch
   - Datasets: Basic electronics for kids, simple circuits

9. **Finance - Money Basics** (2% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: What is money?
   - **Simple**: Buy and sell (trade)
   - **Simple**: Saving (piggy bank)
   - **Simple**: More/less money (counting coins)
   - Datasets: Children's financial literacy, basic economics

10. **Data Science - Counting & Patterns** (2% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: Count things (how many?)
   - **Simple**: Which is bigger? (comparison)
   - **Simple**: Patterns (AB AB AB pattern)
   - **Simple**: Sorting (big to small, colors)
   - Datasets: Basic data literacy, children's statistics

11. **Cooking - Food Basics** (2% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: Raw vs cooked (heat changes food)
   - **Simple**: Hot/cold food (temperature)
   - **Simple**: Ingredients (what food is made of)
   - **Simple**: Taste (sweet, salty, sour, bitter)
   - Datasets: Children's cooking, basic food science

12. **Current Affairs - World Around Us** (1% of phase) 🆕
   - **Simple**: People and places (different countries exist)
   - **Simple**: Weather and seasons
   - **Simple**: Community helpers (firefighter, teacher, doctor)
   - Datasets: Children's news, simple current events

### Modality Mix:
- **Images**: 50% (visual learning dominant in infancy)
- **Audio**: 20% (language sounds, environmental)
- **Text**: 25% (simple sentences, 1-2 syllable words)
- **Video**: 5% (basic motion understanding)

### Success Metrics:
- ✓ Basic object recognition (50+ categories)
- ✓ Simple cause-effect associations
- ✓ Basic word-meaning mappings (500+ words)
- ✓ Low epistemic violations (accepting foundational facts)

### Consolidation Checkpoints:
- **Checkpoint 1.1** (500K examples): Basic sensory patterns
- **Checkpoint 1.2** (1M examples): Simple concepts solidified
- **Checkpoint 1.3** (1.5M examples): Ready for language expansion

## 💤 CONSOLIDATION (Sleep) SCHEDULE

### Consolidation Triggers:
- **Time-based**: Every N examples (varies by phase)
- **Domain-switch**: When switching major domains
- **Cognitive load**: When working memory saturated
- **End of session**: Always consolidate before shutdown

### Consolidation Actions:
1. Transfer high-salience items from working memory to long-term
2. Strengthen important connections (Hebbian learning)
3. Prune weak/redundant connections
4. Integrate new knowledge with existing schemas
5. Reset cognitive load counters

## 📊 DOMAIN ROTATION STRATEGY

### Strategy: "Interleaved Learning"
Research shows interleaved (mixed) practice superior to blocked practice

### Implementation:
- **Never train single domain for >1000 consecutive examples**
- **Rotate domains every 100-500 examples**
- **Weight rotation by phase priorities**
- **Ensure all domains get minimum exposure each session**

### Example Rotation (Phase 3):
```
Philosophy (100) → Chemistry (50) → Law (75) → Rhetoric (100) →
Politics (80) → Religion (60) → Philosophy (120) → Oceanography (70) → ...
```
